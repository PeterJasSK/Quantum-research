About Quantum RNG

Quantum RNG is an experimental project that demonstrates the generation of true quantum randomness using real quantum hardware. Instead of relying on traditional pseudo-random algorithms, this system obtains random values from the fundamental indeterminacy of quantum measurements.

⚛️ How It Works

The generator leverages quantum superposition and measurement to produce unbiased random bits. Each value is derived from the collapse of a qubit’s state on an actual quantum processor — accessed through Amazon Braket. These results are then processed, stored in a local database, and made accessible via this web interface and API.

🧩 Quantum Hardware & Platform

The project currently uses the IQM Emerald system, a 54-qubit IQM Crystal Quantum Processing Unit built on superconducting transmon qubits. The qubits are arranged in a square lattice and interconnected through tunable couplers, enabling high connectivity and precise control of entanglement. The hardware supports arbitrary X and Y rotations as native single-qubit gates, while the CZ gate serves as its native two-qubit operation. This configuration provides a versatile and stable platform for generating quantum randomness through controlled measurements and entangled states.

🎯 Project Goals

  • Demonstrate the use of cloud-based quantum computing for random-number generation.
  • Compare quantum randomness to classical pseudo-random algorithms.
  • Provide a web-based interface for testing, analysis, and visualization of results.

🧠 Background

The concept of true randomness in quantum mechanics is deeply tied to Bell’s Theorem and experimental demonstrations of quantum entanglement. Unlike deterministic classical systems, quantum events are intrinsically unpredictable — making them ideal for cryptography, simulation, and secure key generation.

🧠 Technology Stack

  • Django – backend logic and database management
  • Amazon Braket SDK – access to real quantum hardware
  • Matplotlib – visualizing quantum measurement distributions
  • SQLite – local result storage
