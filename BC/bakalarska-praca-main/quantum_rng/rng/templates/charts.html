Explanation of the tests:

  • Frequency (Monobit) Test: This test checks whether the number of 0s and 1s in the bitstream are approximately equal. A balanced number indicates that the sequence does not have a bias toward one bit. A very low p-value (below 0.01) would indicate a significant imbalance.
  • Runs Test: This test examines the occurrence of consecutive sequences of identical bits, called "runs". It measures whether the number of runs of various lengths matches what is expected in a random sequence. Long runs or too few runs would result in a low p-value, indicating non-randomness.
  • Cumulative Sums (Cusum) Test: This test tracks the cumulative sum of bits, assigning +1 for 1s and -1 for 0s, and checks whether this cumulative sum deviates too far from zero. It helps detect persistent bias that may build up over the bitstream. A low p-value indicates that the sequence tends to drift in one direction instead of fluctuating randomly.

Passing all three tests suggests that the bitstream is statistically balanced, has normal run lengths, and does not show persistent bias. These tests measure basic randomness properties; they do not guarantee cryptographic security or high unpredictability.
